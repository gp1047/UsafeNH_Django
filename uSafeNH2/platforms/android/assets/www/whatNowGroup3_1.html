Reporting to Law Enforcement

Sexual assaults should be reported police department that has jurisdiction (i.e., the police department in the town/city where the assault took place).

You may go directly to your local hospital and request to speak with an officer.

If you do not wish to go to the hospital, officer can meet with you in a safe place to take a brief, initial report. The officer may also encourage you to seek medical attention to ensure that you receive proper care and follow-up.

A person who is 18 years old or older and has been sexually assaulted in NH can contact law enforcement to ask questions about the reporting process without identifying themselves in an effort to determine if they want to come forward and make a formal report. They may also provide some information about what happened to them; however, law enforcement cannot proceed with an investigation without an identified victim.

It is your right to request to have a confidential advocate from your local crisis center accompany you to either the local police department or hospital.
