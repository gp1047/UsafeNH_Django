Deciding whether or not to seek medical attention

Hospitals can provide you with a range of medical care and collect evidence that can be used if you decide to report to police. You or your insurance company will not be charged.

A qualified Sexual Assault Nurse Examiner (SANE) is available at most hospitals to examine and treat any injuries you may have, even if you do not directly observe any injuries at this time. They can provide emergency contraception and medication to prevent certain Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs).

You may bring a friend, family member, or someone you trust with you for support if you would like. A confidential advocate from your local crisis center will also meet with you at the hospital.
