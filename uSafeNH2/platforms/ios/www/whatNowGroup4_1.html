What now?

Support

Healing from a sexual assault can take time, but you do not have to go through this alone. Advocates from your local crisis centercan be reached anytime of the day or night (24/7, 365 days a year). They provide free and confidential support, advocacy, and information, no matter how long ago the assault happened – you do not have to be in crisis to call a crisis center. Advocates are trained individuals who can offer assistance in moments of crisis, as well as resources for ongoing support related to sexual violence. Crisis center services are free of charge.

Speaking with a counselor on campus or in your community can aid in the healing process. Licensed professionals are trained in helping individuals cope in the aftermath of trauma.

You may also wish to open up to a friend or family member that you trust.

Whatever you decide, please remember the following as you continue on your path to healing:

• What happened was not your fault.

• There is no wrong way to respond or feel.

• You deserve to feel safe and respected.

• You are not alone.

• Help is available.

• It is never too late to reach out for support.
