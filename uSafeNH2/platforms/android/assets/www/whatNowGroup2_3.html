What now?

Deciding not to seek medical attention:

Whether or not you wish to seek medical care is entirely your choice. After a sexual assault, many people just want to go home.

Some people change their minds at a later time. While it is best to go as soon as you are able to preserve evidence, you may have a forensic exam up to 5 days after an assault. You or your insurance company will not be charged, and you can have the exam done anonymously if you do not want to make a report at this time.

A qualified Sexual Assault Nurse Examiner (SANE) is available at most hospitals to examine and treat any injuries you may have, even if you do not directly observe any injuries at this time. They can provide emergency contraception and medication to prevent certain Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs).

If you would like to learn more about what happens at the hospital or what your options are:

Speak with an advocate from your local Crisis Center (Available 24/7)
